FYP: The Little Guardian

Can we design games that adapt to who the player is? For my FYP, I dived into the intersection of player traits and mechanical agency. The Little Guardian is a 3D side-scrolling platformer-adventure set in a decaying medieval world. It serves as the primary artifact for my undergraduate thesis, which investigates how game mechanics and player psychology intertwine to create deep engagement.

Final Year Project

The Little Guardian

Title: To Investigate the Relationship Between Player Traits, Actual Mechanical Agency(AMA) and Engagement In Platformer-Adventure Game

Game Trailer: The Little Guardian | 2.5D Side-Scrolling Platformer-Adventure Game

Game Project Overview

The Little Guardian is a 3D side-scrolling platformer-adventure set in a medieval, mysterious world. Players inhabit the role of a young guardian tasked with uncovering the source of a dark curse draining the life from a sacred forest. By blending platforming mechanics with RPG elements, the game invites players to explore a semi-open world filled with secrets, environmental puzzles, and combat challenges. To save the kingdom, players must master fluid movement and strategic problem-solving to confront the decay at the heart of the forest.

Development Process

Storyboarding

Mapped out key emotional beats to ensure the 2-minute duration maintains a strong narrative arc and pacing.

Character Design

Designed the protagonist with oversized eyes to enhance the "innocence lost" theme and evoke stronger empathy from the audience.

Lighting & Shading

Used dynamic shadows in the desert scenes to emphasize the oppressive heat and the character's vulnerability.

Sound Scaping

Layered ambient wind noises with a minimalist piano score to heighten the sense of isolation before the climactic rescue.

Research Impact

From Data to Design

The Hypothesis

Does giving players more "Actual Agency" (AMA) truly make them stay longer in a 3D world?

The Result

Higher AMA significantly increases engagement. Players who felt their actions mattered were 2x more likely to finish the level.

HCI Application

I used these findings to polish The Little Guardian’s movement physics, ensuring every jump felt earned, not scripted.
