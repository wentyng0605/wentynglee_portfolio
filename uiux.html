Building a Design-Centric Portfolio

Role: UI/UX Designer & Front-end Developer

The Challenge

As a Digital Media Technology graduate, , I needed a platform that didn't just 'list' my work, but acted as living proof of my technical proficiency. The goal was to build a high-performance, responsive site from scratch that balances visual storytelling with clean, production-ready code.

The Process

Information Architecture: I categorized my diverse projects (UI/UX, Game Dev, Research) into a modular system, ensuring users can navigate between domains without feeling overwhelmed.

Performance Engineering: Moving away from heavy templates, I chose a vanilla HTML/CSS/JS stack to ensure sub-second loading times and full creative control over micro-interactions.

Design Strategy & HCI Implementation

To ensure an intuitive user journey, I applied Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) principles to solve specific pain points identified during stakeholder interviews.

Immersive Dark Mode Aesthetic

Logic: I implemented a deep-themed UI to serve as a neutral "canvas." This allows the vibrant colors of my project thumbnails to stand out, minimizing visual noise and directing the user's focus to the work.

The Modular Card System

Logic: To ensure scalability, I developed a reusable Card Component. Each card encapsulates project metadata, tech stacks, and visual previews into a single, digestible unit for effortless browsing.

Fluid Responsive Layout (CSS Grid/Flexbox)

Logic: Utilizing a mobile-first approach, I engineered a responsive grid that adapts seamlessly from 4K monitors to mobile screens, maintaining a perfect visual hierarchy at every breakpoint.

Engaging Micro-Interactions

The Logic: Adhering to UX Motion Principles, I added subtle hover effects and smooth transitions. These provide immediate feedback, guiding the user's eye and adding a layer of "polish" that defines high-end web experiences.

UI/UX Case Study

Bloom-In-Motion: Ticketing Web App

Role: UI/UX Designer

The Challenge

Managing event registration for university clubs can be chaotic. Traditional manual sign-ups often lead to fragmented information and high cognitive load for students. The goal was to design a mobile-friendly ticketing web app that simplifies seat selection and payment verification, ensuring a seamless experience for both organizers and attendees.

The Process

Requirement Gathering: I conducted user interviews with club representatives to map out pain points in the existing registration flow, identifying "information clarity", "registration traffic" and "seat availability" as top priorities.

Flow Optimization: Applying Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) principles, I designed an intuitive user flow that minimizes the number of steps required to complete a booking.

Design Strategy & HCI Implementation

To ensure an intuitive user journey, I applied Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) principles to solve specific pain points identified during stakeholder interviews.

Conversion-Optimized Landing Page

Problem: I curated event details and club background information to ensure students can immediately find the "Why" and "What" of the event.

Solution: I designed a high-contrast hero section that highlights the Call-to-Action (Buy Ticket) immediately, paired with a clean information hierarchy for event specifics.

Interactive Seat Selection (Visual Mapping)

Logic: Applied Fitts's Law to optimize button placement and sizing. The auditorium layout uses color-coding to minimize cognitive load, allowing users to select seats with a single tap.

Frictionless Payment Journey

Logic: Implemented Progressive Disclosure in the checkout flow. By breaking down the form into logical steps, I prevented "form fatigue" and reduced potential drop-off rates during payment.

Instant System Feedback

The Logic: Adhering to Nielsen’s Usability Heuristics (Visibility of System Status), I designed a visually distinct confirmation screen to provide immediate closure and peace of mind after a successful transaction.

UI/UX Case Study

TravelGo: Redesign Travel Apps

Role: UX Researcher & UI Designer (HCI Academic Project)

The Challenge

Travelers often struggle to balance itinerary logistics with budget constraints. My goal for this HCI project was to design a seamless management solution that integrates hotel/flight booking with real-time budget tracking, validated through rigorous user testing.

User Research & Insights

I conducted quantitative research with 25 participants to measure usability and user satisfaction.

User Demographic: Target audience aged 18-25, focusing on tech-savvy frequent travelers.

Testing Setup: A Within-Subject Design where users performed specific tasks (Login, Explore Navigation, and Feedback) to test the app's intuitiveness.

Design Strategy & Heuristic Evaluation

Leveraging Nielsen’s 10 Usability Heuristics, I ensured the interface feels familiar and reliable.

Recognition Over Recall (The Dashboard)

Logic: I prioritized clear iconography and visual cues (Hotel, Flights, Food) to reduce the user's cognitive load. Instead of remembering where features are, users can intuitively recognize actions.

System Match to the Real World (Map Integration)

Logic: The map interface utilizes familiar gestures (Pinch-to-zoom, Drag-to-move). The booking flow mimics a real-world logical sequence: choose dates -> select destination -> review options.

Consistency & Standards

Logic: I maintained a strict visual language across the app—from the forest-green color palette to the card-based layout—ensuring users feel confident and comfortable as they navigate.

The Results & Discussion

The prototype was evaluated using the System Usability Scale (SUS) to ensure quantitative validation of the design.
